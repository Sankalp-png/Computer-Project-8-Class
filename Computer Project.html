Stars üåü‚≠ê

Stars are glowing, spherical objects, full of matter called plasma. Plasma is the 4th state of matter after solid, liquid, and gas. All stars are made of two gases- hydrogen(H2) and helium(He). The hydrogen accounts for 75%, while the helium makes up 25% of the matter of a star. As a star ages, hydrogen slowly converts to helium. Thus an old star will have greater amounts of helium.

Our Solar System

Sun
Around 5000 million years back the Sun was formed from a cloud of gas and dust. It consistes of mainly hydrogen (74%) and helium (24%).

Fun Facts about our SUN!
  • The sun contains 99.86 per cent of the total mass of the entire Solar System!!!!!
  • When millions of years from now the sun would have completed the Red Giant phase it will become a white drawf, which would be the same size as of the earth!!!!!

The sun has 8 planets revolving around it. They are-
  • Mercury
    Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is the smallest planet in the Solar system, with a diameter of only 4879 km. It is maned after the greek god who was the Messenger from the heavens, Mercury. Mercury has very long days, as it spins very slowly on its axis, as compared to earth. Mercury has the most number of craters among all the planets in the Solar System. It is very difficult to visit Mercury beacuse of its proximity to the Sun. Up till now only two space crafts have gone to Mercury, the Mariner 10 in 1974-75 and the Messenger probe in 2011-13. It doesn't have any MOONS!!!
  • Venus
    Venus is the second planet from the sun and the hotest of all the planets. Venus is named after the Roman godess of love and beauty. It is known as the earth's sister planets because of their similar size and mass. Venus rotates in a direction opposite to most other planets (from east to west). This means that the sun rises in the west and sets in the east! Venus is known as the morning and the evening star. Till now, 38 space spacecrafts have been sent to Venus. The first mission was sent by the Russians in 1961, the Venera 1. It was followed by the US probe, Mariner 1. However, Mariner 2 was more successful.
  • Earth
    The Earth, our home, is the third planet from the sun and is the largest of the terrestial planets. It was formed around 4.54 billion years ago. It is very much unique, and so much coincidences happened, we are still in a doubt that how all the features which can support life happened so intricately. The presence of water and other conditions which suppory life have transformed Earth from a molten planet to its present form. There's nothing much to say about this wonder. It has one natural sattelite, the Moon, which is the fifth largest sattelite in the solar system after the Galilean moons.
    • The Moon

      The Moon is a spherical body made up of hard rocks and is covered with dust. It is the only natural satellite of Earth. It was formed some 30-50 million years after the formation of our solar system. The size of the moon is one-quarter the size of the Earth.
  • Mars
    Mars is the second smallest planet in our solar system and is fourth planet from the Sun. The name is derived from the Roman god of war. It experiencesthe largest dust storms of all the planets in our solar system. Mars is home to the tallest mountain in our solar system, Olympus Mons. Mars was first discovered by Egyptian astronomers in the 2nd millenium BCE. Though 40 mission have been planned to Mars, only 18 missions have been successful. For instance our country, India, we should be proud to say that was the first nation in the world to be successful in putting a sattelite in the Martian orbit, by ISRO in September 2014 (MOM Mangalyan mission). It has two moons- Phobos and Deimos.
  • Jupiter
    Jupiter is the 5th planet from the Sun and is the 4th brightest object in our Solar System. It is the largest planet, it spins very fast on its axis, hence it experiences the shortest day in our Solar System. Jupiter is made up of rock, metal and hydrogen compounds. Jupiter, just like Venus, is visible to the naked eye from Earth. Jupiter has more than 50 moons. These are called Jovian moons. Jupiter's largest moon discovered by Galileo, Ganymede is the largest moon in the Solar System, which is even larger than Mercury! As many as eight space crafts have visited this planet. These are: Pioneer 10 and 11, Voyager 1 and 2, Galileo, Cassini, Ulysses and, New Horizons. The most recent was the Juno spacecraft.
  • Saturn
    Saturn is the second largest planet, and the sixth planet from the Sun. It is most well-known for its amazing ring system which was first noticed by astronomer Galileo Galilei. Saturn is composed of mixture of gases, like hydrogen and helium. It has a density even lower than water. This means that it will float on water! Saturn is the 5th object in the solar system which can be seen clearly using just a small telescope. Its present name comes from the Roman gad, Saturnus, who is the father of Jupiter the king of Gods. Saturn is the flattest planet of the solar system. Saturn is best known for its extensive ring system. Only 4 spacecrafts have gone to Saturn. The most recent one was the Cassini-Huygens mission. It has around 67 moons, of which Titan, the 2nd largest moon in the Solar System, is the most famous.
  • Uranus
    Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, and is double the distance from the Sun as compared to Saturn. It was the first planet to be discovered with the help of a telescope as it is not visible to the naked eye. Sir William Herschel was the person who discovered Uranus in 1781. Uranus has a mass equal to 15 earths. Uranus is the coldest planet in the Solar System.
  • Neptune
    Neptune is the eighth and farthest-known Solar planet from the Sun. In the Solar System, it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet. It is 17 times the mass of Earth, slightly more massive than its near-twin Uranus.
